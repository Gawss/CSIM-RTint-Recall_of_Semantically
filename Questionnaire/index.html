Thanks for participating.

The objective of this experiment was to determine the impact of semantic relatedness between acoustic and visual stimuli on working memory through retrieval of the stimuli pairs. To do that, the experiment employs a within-subject design, comparing multiple pairs with different levels of relatedness. To ensure that you don’t start remembering stimuli pairs consciously and through rehearsal, we were not able to tell you at the start of the session, that you’ll need to conduct a memory test. We hope you understand that. For each participant, we will calculate the within-subject ANOVA between the proportion of recalled words. If the experimental hypothesis is supported, there will be a significant difference in recall between highly un- and related pairs. The null hypothesis is that there will be no significant difference. Once again, the data you provide will be recorded anonymously and your participation and anything you say during the session will be held in the strictest confidence.
